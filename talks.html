Invited Talks
The Role of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Early Universe Evolution
Venue: International Conference on High Energy Physics, Geneva, Switzerland
Date: July 20, 2023
This talk delves into the formation and properties of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and its crucial role in the evolution of the early universe. The session covered the experimental results from the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and their implications for our understanding of QGP.
Magnetic Fields in Noncentral Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions
Venue: Quark Matter 2024, Heidelberg, Germany
Date: April 15, 2024
This talk focused on the generation of strong transient magnetic fields in noncentral relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It discussed the resulting anisotropy in the strongly interacting medium and the theoretical predictions versus experimental observations.
